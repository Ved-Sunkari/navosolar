Welcome to Nova Solar

Solar energy
through community trust

Nova Solar is a social venture bringing affordable, quality solar products to off-grid households in Uganda's West Nile region — a geography where existing models have failed and grid electricity remains years away for most families.

We work through Village Savings and Loan Associations (VSLAs): trusted community groups where members save together, lend to each other, and now — access clean energy through a distribution model built on relationships, not just transactions.

  • Community-Based Trainers introduce solar products in weekly VSLA meetings
  • Accountable local agents handle sales, inventory, and after-care
  • VSLA loan funds enable purchases for families who can't pay cash upfront
  • Products are VeraSol-certified for quality you can trust

The Issue

Energy poverty in
West Nile

Over half a million households in northern Uganda have no electricity. They spend $6–15 a month on kerosene — up to 25% of a subsistence family's income — for dim, dangerous light.

No grid connection in sight

While a transmission line reached Arua town in 2024, last-mile household connection remains years away for most rural families. Kerosene is the only option — a recurring cost that traps families in energy poverty.

PAYG solar doesn't reach here

Pay-As-You-Go solar — the dominant model in Uganda — requires mobile money, stable income history, and national ID. Refugee families and the most cash-constrained households are systematically excluded.

Agent models have failed before

In 2011, GIZ tested freelance solar agents in Arua — and failed. 46 lamps went unrecovered. Not from dishonesty, but from structurally flawed consignment to capital-constrained agents without community accountability.

Intent exists — fulfillment doesn't

A 2025 randomised study confirmed VSLAs are powerful for building purchase intent — increasing solar savings goals by 32 percentage points. But without a trusted, local fulfillment channel, intent alone doesn't become ownership.

Our Model

How Nova Solar works

We combine what previous models kept separate: community trust infrastructure and accountable, local commercial delivery — the first time these two have been united in this region.

01
Trust Layer

Community-Based Trainer

A CARE-trained facilitator who has worked with VSLA members for months or years runs an information session at a weekly meeting — demonstrating the product from personal experience, not as a salesperson.

02
Commerce Layer

Local Commercial Agent

A community member — often a VSLA member themselves — purchases inventory upfront (no consignment), handles transactions, and answers questions at every weekly meeting. Social accountability replaces absent legal enforcement.

03
Finance Layer

VSLA Loan Fund

Members who can't pay cash upfront can apply for a productive-use loan from their own savings group — no mobile money, no external credit check, no refugee ID barriers. The group's near-100% repayment rate speaks for itself.

Our Impact Goals

Changing lives, measurably

Every pilot we run produces publishable findings. Even setbacks become data that advances the field. Here's what we're working toward.

30%+

Conversion Rate Target

Our pilot success criterion: 30% or more of households who attend a demonstration session make a purchase within 30 days.

75K–125K

Reachable Households

VSLA-affiliated households in West Nile alone — a ready network of organised, financially disciplined families.

$1–2M

Month 60 Revenue Goal

Our 5-year roadmap: pilot in one sub-county, scale to West Nile, expand to Northern Uganda, and replicate across East Africa.

60%

Subsidy Available Now

Uganda's UECCC/EASP program (launched November 2024) discounts certified solar lanterns by 60% for registered distributors — dramatically expanding affordability.

"They are currently spending a disproportionate share of income on kerosene and have explicitly demonstrated willingness to save for solar products when supported by a structured savings mechanism."
— Elven et al., 2025 · Randomised Control Trial, Uganda VSLA Groups
