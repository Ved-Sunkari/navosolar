Welcome to Navo Solar

Solar energy
through community trust

Navo Solar is a social venture bringing affordable, quality solar products to off-grid households in Uganda's West Nile region — a geography where existing models have failed and grid electricity remains years away.

We work through Village Savings and Loan Associations (VSLAs): trusted community groups where members save together, lend to each other, and now — access clean energy through a distribution model built on relationships, not just transactions.

  • Community-Based Trainers introduce solar products at weekly VSLA meetings
  • Accountable local agents handle sales, inventory, and after-care
  • VSLA loan funds enable purchases for families who can't pay cash upfront
  • All products are VeraSol-certified for quality you can trust

The Issue

Energy poverty in
West Nile

Over half a million households in northern Uganda have no electricity. They spend $6–15 a month on kerosene — up to 25% of a subsistence family's income — for dim, dangerous light.

No grid connection in sight

While a transmission line reached Arua town in 2024, last-mile household connection remains years away for most rural families. Kerosene is the only option — a recurring cost that traps families in energy poverty.

PAYG solar doesn't reach here

Pay-As-You-Go solar requires mobile money, stable income history, and national ID. Refugee families and the most cash-constrained households are systematically excluded from every existing model.

Agent models have failed before

In 2011, GIZ tested freelance solar agents in Arua — and failed. 46 lamps went unrecovered. Not from dishonesty, but from structurally flawed consignment to capital-constrained agents without accountability.

Intent exists — fulfillment doesn't

A 2025 randomised study confirmed VSLAs increase solar savings goals by 32 percentage points. But without a trusted, local fulfillment channel, intent alone doesn't become ownership.

Our Model

How Navo Solar works

We combine what previous models kept separate: community trust infrastructure and accountable, local commercial delivery — the first time these two have been united in this region.

01
Trust Layer

Community-Based Trainer

A CARE-trained facilitator who has worked with VSLA members for months or years runs an information session at a weekly meeting — demonstrating the product from personal experience, not as a salesperson.

02
Commerce Layer

Local Commercial Agent

A community member — often a VSLA member themselves — purchases inventory upfront (no consignment), handles transactions, and answers questions at every weekly meeting. Social accountability replaces absent legal enforcement.

03
Finance Layer

VSLA Loan Fund

Members who can't pay cash upfront apply for a productive-use loan from their own savings group — no mobile money, no external credit check, no refugee ID barriers. The group's near-100% repayment rate speaks for itself.
